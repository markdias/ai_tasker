# Example Prompts & Expected Task Generation

This guide shows real-world prompts and the types of tasks you should expect to be generated.

## Event Planning Examples

### Prompt: "Plan my daughter's birthday party"
**Expected Tasks**:
1. **Create Guest List** (list type)
   - Fields: Guest Name, Email, Phone, Dietary Restrictions

2. **Plan Birthday Menu** (list type)
   - Fields: Dish Name, Type (Appetizer/Main/Dessert), Servings, Cost Per Serving

3. **Cake Selection & Customization** (text type)
   - Choose flavor, design, dietary accommodations

4. **Party Decorations** (list type)
   - Fields: Item, Quantity, Estimated Cost, Color/Theme

5. **Entertainment & Activities** (list type)
   - Fields: Activity Name, Duration, Participant Count, Cost

6. **Party Supplies Checklist** (list type)
   - Fields: Item, Quantity Needed, Purchased, Cost

7. **Set Party Budget** (currency type)
   - Total budget allocation across all categories

8. **Timeline & Schedule** (date type)
   - Event date, prep deadlines, decoration setup time

## How to Use These Examples

1. **Try similar prompts** with your own context
2. **Customize your answers** to the clarifying questions
3. **Expect 5-8 tasks** per project
4. **Look for list tasks** for any multi-item tracking needs
5. **Edit as needed** before saving tasks
6. **Add costs** to tasks for budget tracking
7. **Set due dates** for task accountability
8. **Assign categories** for better organization

## Tips for Best Results

✅ **Be specific** - "Plan a 50-person company picnic" generates better tasks than "Plan an event"

✅ **Include context** - Answer the clarifying questions thoroughly

✅ **Think about scope** - Include budget, timeline, and important details

✅ **Review before accepting** - Edit task titles and descriptions to match your vision

✅ **Add details** - Use list fields to capture all necessary information

✅ **Set budgets** - Enable cost tracking for financial accountability

✅ **Use categories** - Tag related tasks for easy filtering
